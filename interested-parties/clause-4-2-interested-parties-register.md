## 1. Purpose & Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document establishes the **Interested Parties Register** in accordance with **ISO 27001:2022 Clause 4.2 – Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties**. The purpose of this register is to:

- Identify all internal and external interested parties relevant to EuroTrust Bank AG's Information Security Management System (ISMS)
- Document the information security requirements and expectations of each interested party
- Define how these requirements will be addressed within the ISMS framework
- Establish communication methods and review mechanisms for ongoing engagement

### 1.2 Scope

This register covers all interested parties relevant to EuroTrust Bank AG's ISMS, including:
- Internal stakeholders across all organizational levels
- External regulators and supervisory authorities
- Customers and clients (retail and corporate)
- Business partners, suppliers, and service providers
- Oversight and assurance bodies
- Financial stakeholders
- Industry associations and the public

**Context:** EuroTrust Bank AG is a fictional European banking institution headquartered in Frankfurt, Germany, with branches in Paris, Amsterdam, Madrid, Warsaw, and Dublin. The bank employs approximately 5,000 staff and serves ~2 million retail customers and ~10,000 corporate clients through a hybrid cloud infrastructure.

**Note:** This document is prepared for educational and training purposes as part of an ISO 27001 audit simulation. EuroTrust Bank AG is a fictional entity.

### 1.3 Reference Standards

- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 – Information Security Management Systems – Requirements
- ISO/IEC 27002:2022 – Information Security Controls
- ISO 19011:2018 – Guidelines for Auditing Management Systems

## 3. Summary of Key Themes

The requirements of interested parties can be grouped into the following key themes, which drive ISMS controls and processes:

### 3.1 Data Protection and Privacy
- **Interested Parties:** DPO, National DPAs, Retail Customers, Corporate Clients
- **Key Requirements:** GDPR compliance, data subject rights, breach notification, privacy by design, cross-border data transfers
- **ISMS Response:** Privacy governance framework, DPIA procedures, data protection controls, breach response procedures, BCR implementation

### 3.2 Operational Resilience and Business Continuity
- **Interested Parties:** ECB/SSM, BaFin, Board, Executive Management, Customers
- **Key Requirements:** Service availability, disaster recovery, business continuity, incident response, recovery time objectives
- **ISMS Response:** Business continuity management system, disaster recovery plans, resilience testing, incident response procedures, high availability architecture

### 3.3 Regulatory Compliance and Incident Reporting
- **Interested Parties:** BaFin, ECB, National Regulators, ENISA/BSI, DPAs
- **Key Requirements:** Timely incident reporting (72 hours for significant incidents), regulatory compliance, supervisory cooperation, audit rights
- **ISMS Response:** Incident classification and reporting procedures, regulatory compliance program, supervisory reporting framework, audit readiness

### 3.4 Third-Party Risk Management
- **Interested Parties:** Cloud Providers (AWS), Outsourcing Providers, SWIFT, Payment Networks
- **Key Requirements:** Vendor security assessments, contractual security requirements, right to audit, breach notification, exit strategies
- **ISMS Response:** Third-party risk management framework, vendor due diligence procedures, ongoing monitoring, contract security clauses, EBA outsourcing guidelines compliance

### 3.5 Access Control and Authentication
- **Interested Parties:** IT Operations, Corporate Clients, Retail Customers, Internal Audit
- **Key Requirements:** Multi-factor authentication, least privilege, segregation of duties, audit logging, privileged access management
- **ISMS Response:** Identity and access management (IAM) framework, MFA implementation, role-based access control, privileged access management (PAM), comprehensive audit logging

### 3.6 Cyber Threat Intelligence and Information Sharing
- **Interested Parties:** ENISA, BSI, ANSSI, Banking Associations, Law Enforcement
- **Key Requirements:** Threat intelligence sharing, vulnerability disclosure, security advisories, cybercrime reporting
- **ISMS Response:** Threat intelligence program, information sharing agreements, vulnerability management, security operations center (SOC), CERT coordination

### 3.7 Governance, Accountability, and Oversight
- **Interested Parties:** Board, Executive Management, Internal Audit, Shareholders
- **Key Requirements:** Clear accountability, board-level oversight, management review, performance metrics, risk reporting
- **ISMS Response:** ISMS governance structure, management review process, KPI dashboard, board reporting procedures, roles and responsibilities matrix

### 3.8 Security Awareness and Training
- **Interested Parties:** All Employees, HR, IT Operations
- **Key Requirements:** Security awareness training, role-based training, phishing simulations, security culture
- **ISMS Response:** Security awareness program, mandatory annual training, role-specific training, phishing simulations, security communications

### 3.9 Financial and Payment Security
- **Interested Parties:** SWIFT, Payment Card Networks, Correspondent Banks, Corporate Clients
- **Key Requirements:** PCI DSS compliance, SWIFT CSP compliance, transaction security, fraud prevention
- **ISMS Response:** PCI DSS compliance program, SWIFT CSP implementation, transaction monitoring, fraud detection systems

### 3.10 Transparency and Communication
- **Interested Parties:** Media, General Public, Customers, Shareholders
- **Key Requirements:** Responsible disclosure, incident transparency, reputation management, customer communication
- **ISMS Response:** Communications strategy, incident disclosure procedures, customer notification processes, PR coordination

## 4. Review & Maintenance

### 4.1 Regular Review Frequency

This Interested Parties Register shall be formally reviewed and updated according to the following schedule:

- **Quarterly Review:** Conducted by CISO Office to ensure ongoing relevance
- **Annual Comprehensive Review:** Conducted during Management Review as part of ISMS annual assessment
- **Post-Incident Review:** When significant security incidents occur that may affect interested party requirements

### 4.2 Triggers for Ad-Hoc Review

The register shall be reviewed and updated immediately when any of the following triggers occur:

1. **Regulatory Changes:** New or amended laws, regulations, or supervisory guidance affecting information security
2. **Organizational Changes:** Mergers, acquisitions, new branches, significant changes in business model
3. **Material Incidents:** Security breaches or incidents that affect interested parties
4. **New Interested Parties:** Identification of new stakeholders relevant to the ISMS
5. **Contract Changes:** Significant changes to contracts with critical suppliers or customers
6. **Strategic Changes:** Changes in business strategy, new products/services, or technology platforms
7. **Audit Findings:** Internal or external audit findings that identify gaps in interested party management
8. **Certification/Accreditation:** Changes related to ISO 27001 certification or other accreditations

### 4.3 Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **CISO** | Overall ownership of the register; approval of updates; escalation of significant changes to executive management |
| **Information Security Manager** | Day-to-day maintenance; coordination of quarterly reviews; stakeholder consultation; documentation updates |
| **Compliance Officer** | Input on regulatory changes; monitoring legal/regulatory landscape; validation of compliance requirements |
| **Risk Manager** | Integration with enterprise risk register; risk assessment of interested party requirements; validation of risk treatment |
| **Business Unit Heads** | Input on business-specific interested parties; validation of requirements; communication of changes |
| **Management Representative** | Review and endorsement during management review; ensuring integration with other ISMS processes |

### 4.4 Communication of Changes

Significant changes to the Interested Parties Register shall be communicated to:
- Executive Management (via monthly ISMS steering committee)
- ISMS process owners (via quarterly coordination meetings)
- Internal Audit (via quarterly audit coordination)
- All employees (via internal communications when affecting general staff)

### 4.5 Documentation

All reviews and updates shall be documented with:
- Date of review
- Reviewer(s)
- Changes made and rationale
- Approval and effective date
- Version control in document header
