## Section B: True or False Questions (Questions 26-30)

### Question 26
ISO 27001:2022 requires implementation of all 93 Annex A controls.

**Answer: False** - Organizations must justify which controls are applicable through the Statement of Applicability.

---

### Question 27
Risk appetite and risk tolerance are the same concepts in risk management.

**Answer: False** - Risk appetite is the amount of risk an organization is willing to accept, while risk tolerance is the acceptable variation around objectives.

---

### Question 28
Top management must demonstrate leadership and commitment to the ISMS.

**Answer: True** - This is a requirement in Clause 5.1 of ISO 27001:2022.

---

### Question 29
An organization can achieve ISO 27001 certification without conducting internal audits.

**Answer: False** - Internal audits are mandatory as per Clause 9.2.

---

### Question 30
Information security objectives must be measurable.

**Answer: True** - As stated in Clause 6.2, objectives must be measurable when practicable.

## Section C: Scenario-Based Questions (Questions 31-40)

### Question 31-33: Data Breach Scenario

**Scenario**: A financial services company experienced a data breach where customer credit card information was accessed by an unauthorized party. The investigation revealed that a former employee's access credentials were still active six months after termination. The company has ISO 27001 certification.

#### Question 31
Which Annex A control category was most likely not effectively implemented?

A) Physical controls  
B) People controls  
C) Technological controls  
D) Organizational controls

**Answer: B** - People controls, specifically related to termination or change of employment.

---

#### Question 32
What type of nonconformity would this likely be classified as?

A) Minor nonconformity  
B) Major nonconformity  
C) Observation  
D) Opportunity for improvement

**Answer: B** - Failure to revoke access after termination is a significant control failure.

---

#### Question 33
Which immediate corrective action should the auditor expect?

A) Update the information security policy  
B) Conduct security awareness training  
C) Immediate revocation of access for all terminated employees  
D) Purchase new access control system

**Answer: C** - Immediate action to prevent further unauthorized access.

---

### Question 34-36: Risk Assessment Scenario

**Scenario**: During an audit, you discover that an organization conducts risk assessments annually. However, the organization recently expanded to a new geographic region with different regulatory requirements, but no new risk assessment was conducted.

#### Question 34
What is the primary concern in this situation?

A) The risk assessment frequency is too low  
B) The risk assessment may not reflect current organizational context  
C) The organization needs external consultants  
D) The risk methodology is incorrect

**Answer: B** - Changes in context should trigger risk reassessment.

---

#### Question 35
Which clause of ISO 27001:2022 is most relevant to this finding?

A) Clause 4 - Context of the organization  
B) Clause 6 - Planning  
C) Clause 8 - Operation  
D) Both A and B

**Answer: D** - Both context and planning are affected.

---

#### Question 36
What would be an appropriate recommendation?

A) Conduct risk assessment every month  
B) Conduct risk assessment when significant changes occur  
C) Hire more staff  
D) Eliminate the new geographic expansion

**Answer: B** - Risk assessment should be triggered by significant changes.

---

### Question 37-38: Control Implementation Scenario

**Scenario**: An organization has documented a comprehensive access control policy but the audit reveals that user access rights are not regularly reviewed. Some users have accumulated excessive privileges over time.

#### Question 37
This situation represents a gap between:

A) Policy and procedure  
B) Procedure and implementation  
C) Documentation and practice  
D) All of the above

**Answer: C** - The policy exists but is not being practiced effectively.

---

#### Question 38
Which type of control failure is this?

A) Preventive control failure  
B) Detective control failure  
C) Corrective control failure  
D) Management control failure

**Answer: D** - Regular access reviews are a management control.

---

### Question 39-40: Management Review Scenario

**Scenario**: You are reviewing management review meeting minutes. You notice that the agenda includes status of information security objectives, audit results, and customer feedback. However, there is no discussion on changes in internal and external issues affecting the ISMS.

#### Question 39
Is this a nonconformity?

A) Yes, major nonconformity  
B) Yes, minor nonconformity  
C) No, but it's an observation  
D) No, the agenda is complete

**Answer: B** - Clause 9.3 requires consideration of changes in internal and external issues.

---

#### Question 40
What should be the auditor's recommendation?

A) Conduct management reviews more frequently  
B) Include discussion on changes affecting ISMS in management reviews  
C) Cancel the management review process  
D) Hire external consultants for management reviews

**Answer: B** - The management review should be comprehensive as per Clause 9.3 requirements.
